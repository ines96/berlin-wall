From the 70s until the end of the century, several artists expressed their opinion regarding the Berlin Wall. Pink Floyd, David Bowie and Scorpions, for example, sang about the German barrier, with some of these songs becoming an unavoidable landmark in history.

Songs List

  • Sex Pistols – “Holidays in the Sun” (1977)

    Considered one of the most controversial English bands ever, the Sex Pistols released this track in 1977, on the band's only album. The music was inspired by Berlin and the division that represented the wall.

  • David Bowie – “Heroes” (1977)

    To the unmistakable voice of David Bowie are added striking songs in a career with more than 40 years. "Heroes", one of the three albums dedicated to the city of Berlin and the single with the same name and which depicts a love story divided by the wall are an example of these songs.

  • Pink Floyd – “Another Brick in the Wall” (1979)

    A band that needs no introduction, Pink Floyd marked history with several songs that have become authentic hymns. "Another Brick in the Wall" is one of the clear examples of the band's impact. Both the track and the album itself owe their name to the Berlin Wall.

  • Scorpions – “Wind of Change” (1991)

    One of the most iconic songs of the last century, both for the symbolic implications present in the lyrics and for the music. “Wind of Change” is marked by a strong opinion about the changes that come from the fall of the Berlin Wall.

  • Pink Floyd – “A great day for freedom” (1994)

    One of the most recent songs to draw inspiration from the Berlin wall. Pink Floyd sing the impact of the fall of the wall on society, criticizing its evolution, the direction that society has taken since that moment.


Times of crisis are always prolific times for art and the fall of the Berlin wall is no exception. The cinema did not ignore the subject and portrayed it from several perspectives, from the thriller to the comedy, through the novel and the suspense.

Movies List

  • “The Legend of Rita” (2000)

    Rita Vogt is a radical West German terrorist who abandons the revolution and settles on the other side of the wall with a new identity. The film must, however, live in the permanent fear of being discovered, which happens after German reunification.

  • “The Tunnel” (2001)

    This film tells the story of Harry Melchior, a swimmer who was arrested for his role in the June 1953 uprising in East Germany, and who decides to dig a tunnel to escape with his family to West Germany.

  • “Good Bye, Lenin!” (2003)

    "Goodbye, Lenine!" accompanies a young man who, in an attempt to protect the health of his socialist mother, after having been in a coma for months, hides the fall of the Berlin wall and the establishment of a capitalist society with the knowledge of it.

  • “Herr Lehmann” (2003)

    Frank Lehmann is a bartender who works in Kreuzberg, alongside a subculture of students, artists and bohemians. The story takes place in the weeks before the fall of the Berlin Wall and close to Frank's 30th birthday, and his calm lifestyle is interrupted by his relationship with Katrin, the madness of his best friend, Karl, and a visit from your parents.
